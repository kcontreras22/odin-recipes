Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

It is rare to find a person that does not like chocolate chip cookies, but they do exist. With this recipe, it is possible for them to finally see the light.

This chocolate chip cookie recipe is best for those that love big and chewy cookies! You will have to be patient though - the dough will need to chill (preferably overnight) for the best results!

Ingredients (yields ~ 11)

    Wet Ingredients
  • 1/2 Cup - Unsalted Butter
  • 1 - Large Egg
  • 3/4 Cup - Light Brown Sugar, Packed
  • 1/4 Cup - Granulated Sugar

    • Dry Ingredients
  • 1 2/3 Cup - All-Purpose Flour
  • 2 tsp - Cornstarch
  • 1 tsp - Baking Soda
  • 1/2 tsp - Salt
  • 1 Cup - Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips

Prep:

  1. Heat butter on high-power to melt, about 1 minute or 30 seconds.
  2. Add the egg, sugar, vanilla to the bowl with the melted butter and whisk to combine.
  3. Add flour, cornstarch, baking soda, salt and stir to combine. Dough may seem crumbly, that's OK.
  4. Add the chocolate chips and stir to combine.
  5. Use a large cookie scoop or a 1/4 Cup measuring cup, form 11-12 sized mounds of dough, roll into balls, place on cookie sheet and flatten halfway.
  6. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours. Preferably overnight. :)

Baking:

  • Pre-heat oven to 350Â°F
  • Make sure mounds are at least 2 inches apart
  • Bake for about 12-14 minutes (if your cookies looks small, reduce baking time) or until edges have set. Even if slightly under-baked, pale, don't overbake. The cookies will firm up as they cool.
